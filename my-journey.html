Bio

I always loved math.

Took a detour.

Returned 5 years after high school with a passion.

Found that pure math and physics was not really what I enjoyed. I actually enjoy empolying math as a tool to solve real-world problems. That is what engineering is,

Engineering is the application of mathematics, physics, and computer science to solve real-world problems.

I switched majors to Electrical Engineering which is very mathematical and has a large intersection with computer science.

I was intrigued by control theory, signal processing and optimization. I enjoyed how the solutions to were always deeply rooted in mathematical theory yet the main focus was always to do something useful.

This is were I got my first taste of computers extending human abilities--in EE we make computers and devices do cool things, but behind the scenes there is always a smart human setting things up and ensuring that things don't go crazy.
