Organizing the Hawaii Machine Learning Meetup

Matt Motoki, Fri 29 June 2018, Fri 29 June 2018, Misc

Meetup

Summary

In July of 2017, my friends Tom Yokota, Sora Khongnawong (Paul) and I decided to create a Meetup centered around data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence in Hawaii. We sought to promte these technologies and increase their adoption in Hawaii.

It took more work than we expected but we now have a vibrant community of 250+ members, we've held periodic study groups for an online machine learning course, and we hosted Hawaii's very first machine learning competition. Overall, I wold consider the Meetup a success.

Motivation

In Hawaii, most people agree that businesses typically adopt technology 5-10 years after the mainland US. This was especially true for data science and machine learning. At the time, Tom and I were both working as data scientists at two of the very few consultancy firm specializing in analytics. At work, one of our biggest hurdles was explaining to our clients what data science and machine learning is and what it isn't, what it could and couldn't do. As frustrating as it was, the lack of understanding of data science and machine learning was understandable; at the time, there was almost no community resources for data science and machine learning--we wanted to change that.

On Jul 19, 2017 we created the Hawaii Machine Learning Meetup. Two days later we had 20+ members. We were underprepared--we didn't have any meetup content lined up and no place to actually host a meetup. All we knew was that we wanted to promote machine learning in Hawaii. After assessing the state of machine learning in Hawaii, we came up with our three main goals for the meetup:

  1. Host talks and presentations given by our members, academics, and people in industry. This was our main role. As organizers, we would work on contributing our own presentations to the meetup, but we want the focus to be on how the community in Hawaii is using machine learning.

  2. Promote an understanding of machine learning through study groups. This was of secondary importance. We planned to organize study groups for members enrolled in or auditing online courses. We also planned to hold a periodic review session for the material covered in the course.

  3. Organize local machine learning competitions & encourage participation in global competitions. We believed that participating in machine learning competitions is a great way to apply machine learning techniques and also a great way to learn new techniques.

Our first Meetup wasn't until September but we once we got started we picked up steam. In October, we brought on a new organizer Sam Murphy. With his help we were able to secure the Manoa Innovation Center as our venue for the meetup. This also meant that we had our first sponsor the Hawaii Technology Development Coporation.

In December we started our study group for the Machine Learning A-Z course on Udemy. To complete the course, we outlined a schedule to meet every Saturday for 4 months to discuss a few sections the course.

On March 30th we kicked off Hawaii's first machine learning competition on the Kaggle In-Class platform. From then until April our members competed to predict the quantity of items sold in online sales transactions. We held 4 tutorial sessions to help newcomers to competitive machine learning.

We've had presentations from our members, researchers from the University of Hawaii's Cancer Research Center, an Assistant Professor in Economics, and even out-of-town guests. In other words, we met our three main goals for the meetup.

Conclusion

At the time I was working as a data analyst at a local startup focusing primarily on fundraising analytics for nonprofits.
